# Domain Taxonomy & Content Organization

## Domain Categories

- Top-level domains represent major industry/application areas: Healthcare, Manufacturing, Logistics & Supply Chain, Retail, Telecommunications, Transportation, Financial Services, Government & Public Sector, Energy, Food & Beverage, etc.
- A model can belong to multiple domains.
- The domain list is editable over time — new domains added as the registry grows, rarely-used ones merged or retired.

## Foundational Patterns

- Some models are fundamentally domain-agnostic — "Single Server Queue," "Parallel Processing with Shared Resources," "Batch-and-Queue."
- A dedicated "Foundational Patterns" or "Classic DES Models" category captures these.
- They serve an educational purpose — the building blocks that more complex domain-specific models are composed from.
- Domain-specific entries can reference foundational patterns they build upon (e.g., "Emergency Department Patient Flow" builds on "Priority Queue with Multiple Servers").

## Curated Collections

- Editor-curated groupings that cut across domains and patterns: "Getting Started with DES," "10 Models Every Practitioner Should Know," "Common Manufacturing Scenarios."
- Collections appear on the homepage and can be browsed as their own section.
- These are the "staff picks" or themed rows — editorial voice and curation.
